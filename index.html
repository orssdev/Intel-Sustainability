Building a Greener Future: Intel’s Sustainability Through the Years

From Silicon to Sustainability: Our Ongoing Commitment

Since 1968, Intel has driven innovation not just in technology, but in environmental responsibility. Explore our journey through the decades as we strive for a more sustainable future.

1971
The First Microprocessor
Intel introduces the world’s first microprocessor, the 4004, launching a new era of computing and energy efficiency.
1995
Environmental Policy
Launch of Intel’s first company-wide environmental policy, focusing on waste reduction and recycling.
2003
Waste Recycling Milestone
Intel achieves 90% recycling of manufacturing waste globally.
2010
Renewable Energy Initiative
Launched first major renewable energy initiative across U.S. campuses.
2012
Water Conservation
Reduced water usage by 30% in manufacturing processes worldwide.
2015
Green Building Expansion
Achieved 75% waste recycling milestone and expanded green building initiatives.
2020
Net-Zero Commitment
Committed to net-zero greenhouse gas emissions by 2040.
2023
Sustainability Education
Launched global sustainability education program for employees and communities.
2025
100% Renewable Energy
Reached 100% renewable energy usage in all Intel facilities worldwide.
